# Title Strategy

## Purpose
This document defines how MargotGameOfLife should handle naming during early development so the repo can stay stable, internal discussion can get clearer, and future public naming can change without causing unnecessary churn.

## Core Naming Rule
Separate internal project naming from future public-facing title naming. Do not treat the current repo slug as the long-term codename or as a premature commercial title.

## Naming Layers
- **Repo Slug**: the current repository identifier. Keep it stable for now to avoid needless churn in docs, runtime placeholders, and references. It may change later, but only for a clear practical reason.
- **Internal Codename**: the studio's preferred day-to-day project name. This should be adopted now, used in planning and discussion, and remain more stable than public-facing experiments.
- **Working Title**: a provisional player-facing title used to explore direction. It should be flexible and may change several times as the game becomes more legible.
- **Future Ship Title**: the eventual commercial title. This should stay deferred until the game has broader surface area, stronger external identity, and better proof of what it actually is.

Stable now:
- repo slug
- internal codename

Flexible later:
- working title
- future ship title

## What the Name Must Carry
- **Warmth**: the name should feel welcoming rather than cold or technical.
- **Wonder**: it should leave room for magic, creatures, and old mystery.
- **Place**: it should feel rooted in a lived-in world, not in a generic genre label.
- **Growth**: it should suggest becoming, tending, building, or maturing over time.
- **Care**: it should fit a game about stewardship, restoration, and shared life.
- **Mystery**: it should hint at depth without becoming dark or obscure.
- **Craft**: it should feel compatible with making, repair, and skilled work.
- **Durability**: it should age well as the game deepens beyond the first slice.

## What the Name Must Avoid
- generic "`life sim`" phrasing that says almost nothing specific
- finance-first framing that makes the game sound like money management
- babyish toyland naming that the player will outgrow too quickly
- hard fantasy cliche naming built from kingdoms, destinies, or ancient war language
- direct dependence on a child or family member's personal name
- titles that cannot age with the player as meaning deepens

## Naming Direction Families
- **Hearth / Home / Village**: strong for warmth, belonging, and shared life. Risk: can become too domestic or too plain if it loses wonder.
- **Grove / Orchard / Meadow**: strongest overall because it carries place, growth, care, and expansion-friendly natural imagery. Risk: can become too soft if not paired with depth or structure.
- **Bridge / Path / Crossing**: strong for connection, public works, and movement through a changing world. Risk: can feel too infrastructural if used alone.
- **Garden / Old Works / Lantern**: strong for restoration, quiet mystery, and crafted atmosphere. Risk: can drift toward decorative poetry if not grounded in place.
- **Wonder / Creature / Light**: strong for child attraction and magical tone. Risk: can become too cute, too vague, or too close to collectible-creature framing if overused.

## Recommended Internal Codename
**Hearthgrove**

This is the best practical codename for now because it carries warmth, place, growth, care, and durability in one compact name. It fits the canon better than a finance, child-name, or generic life-sim label, and it does not overcommit to apples, pies, bridges, or any single early feature.

## Alternate Internal Codenames
- **Hearthvale**: warm and grounded, with slightly broader geographic room than a grove.
- **Grovepath**: strong on place plus movement, with good fit for additive district expansion.
- **Meadowbridge**: directly evokes warmth and connection, though it is more slice-specific than the best option.
- **Willowpath**: soft, memorable, and gently mysterious without sounding childish.
- **Lantern Grove**: carries wonder and old-place atmosphere while staying readable.
- **Orchard Vale**: close to the current slice and very legible, but somewhat narrower in long-term range.
- **Grovehold**: gives more settlement weight and durability without going full fantasy-kingdom.
- **Old Garden**: strong on restoration and ancient depth, though more wistful than infrastructural.

## Working Title Directions
- **The Grove and the Bridge**: directly reflects the first slice while sounding more like a world than a prototype.
- **Hearth in the Grove**: carries warmth and place without sounding childish or generic.
- **The Orchard Bridge**: very legible and concrete, with clear roots in restoration and shared access.
- **Garden of the Old Road**: hints at old works and layered place memory without overexplaining.
- **Lanterns in the Valley**: warmer and more atmospheric, with room for wonder and village life.
- **The Bridge at Grove End**: place-specific and story-ready without locking the full game into plot.
- **The Quiet Grove**: gentle, durable, and wonder-friendly, though less explicit about society.
- **A Village of Paths**: strong on shared life, routes, and additive expansion through places.

## Titles to Reject
- **`Margot's Life Sim`**: too dependent on a personal name and too generic about genre.
- **`Village Tycoon`**: frames the game as shallow accumulation and optimization.
- **`Tiny Unicorn Town`**: too toy-like and too young to age with the player.
- **`Kingdom of the Ancient Grove`**: drifts into generic fantasy-kingdom language the project does not want.
- **`Coins of Community`**: puts finance in the foreground and warmth in the background.
- **`The Philosophy of Care`**: too abstract, too intellectual, and not rooted in place.

Reject these naming patterns in general:
- names that lead with genre instead of world
- names that sound like economy games first
- names that sound like preschool products
- names that borrow fantasy cliche structure to fake depth
- names that need personal-family context to make sense

## Recommendation
- **Keep as-is for now**: `MargotGameOfLife` as the repo slug, plus current runtime and module names.
- **Start using immediately**: `Hearthgrove` as the internal codename in planning, discussion, and future production docs when a codename is useful.
- **Defer until later**: any repo rename and any final public-facing title choice.

## Rename Timing Rule
- **Rename the repo** only after the internal codename has actually stuck in day-to-day use, the first slice is more proven, and the churn cost is worth paying.
- **Rename the internal codename** only if it becomes actively misleading, awkward in practice, or clearly weaker than a better option.
- **Rename the public working title** whenever the playable game gains enough clarity that a better player-facing expression becomes obvious. Keep this flexible for longer.

## Final Rule
Choose names that feel like a place worth growing into.
