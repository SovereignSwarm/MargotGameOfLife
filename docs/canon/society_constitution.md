# Society Constitution

## Purpose
Society in MargotGameOfLife must be learned through lived interdependence. Players should feel shared life by helping build, maintain, and judge it.

## Core Rule
Society must stay concrete. Shared life should be expressed through places, projects, access, routines, and mutual dependence rather than through abstract moral instruction.

## Projects vs Institutions
- **Projects** are the clearest early form of civic life because they show progress, sacrifice, and outcome in one visible arc.
- **Institutions** may appear later, but they should still be embodied in places, people, and repeatable public functions.

Projects are the primary early vehicle because they are child-legible and visibly causal.

## Visible-World Consequence
Visible civic consequence is mandatory. Public contribution must change something players can return to and notice:
- a route opens
- a place improves
- work becomes easier
- safety or beauty increases
- shared opportunity grows

If the player cannot point to the changed thing, the civic system is too abstract.

## Interdependence
The town should matter because people, places, and work depend on one another. Society is not an extra layer on top of the economy; it is part of what gives work meaning.

## Scope Layers
- **Personal**: what one player controls and benefits from directly
- **Shared Household**: what the family or co-op pair governs together
- **Town / Civic**: what exists for common use or common benefit

Good society design makes these scopes legible and makes trade-offs between them meaningful.

## Anti-Patterns
- invisible moral score systems
- public-good mechanics that never change the world
- institutions that exist only as menus or meters
- civic deductions with no felt cause
- lecture-driven "lessons" instead of lived consequence
