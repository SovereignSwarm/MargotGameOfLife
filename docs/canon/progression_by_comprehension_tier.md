# Progression by Comprehension Tier

## Purpose
These tiers describe how the same system should deepen over time without becoming a different game. Use them when designing new mechanics, content, and information layers.

## The Five Tiers
- **Wonder**: immediate cause and effect is visible and satisfying.
- **Planning**: the player can see short future consequences and delay reward on purpose.
- **Strategy**: the player can compare options, opportunity costs, and better sequences.
- **Systems**: the player can read interactions across production, ownership, society, and information.
- **Legacy**: the player can think in enduring patterns about what kind of life, household, and town they are building.

Age and comprehension are related but not identical. A bright young child may reach Strategy in one system while staying at Wonder in another.

## Mapping Rules
- Every important system must be readable at Wonder first.
- Higher tiers should deepen the same system, not replace it with a different abstraction.
- Strategy and Systems must still be grounded in visible world consequence.
- Legacy should come from recurring patterns, not from adding abstract lectures.
- If a feature only works at Systems or Legacy, it is too late for the first introduction.

## Orchard / Apple Tree Example
- **Wonder**: the tree is pretty and gives apples again.
- **Planning**: saving for the tree matters because it helps later.
- **Strategy**: tree output changes whether time should go to gathering, baking, or saving.
- **Systems**: the tree links household planning, market output, and future project contribution.
- **Legacy**: the tree shows how productive ownership changes life beyond one day's labor.

## Pie Crafting Example
- **Wonder**: apples and flour become pie.
- **Planning**: inputs must be gathered before the pie can be sold.
- **Strategy**: crafting time competes with other uses of time and goods.
- **Systems**: pies connect resource flow, trade flow, and cash flow.
- **Legacy**: repeatable craft becomes a stable habit of value creation rather than a one-off trick.

## Bridge / Public Works Example
- **Wonder**: the bridge site changes as the fund grows.
- **Planning**: contributing now delays personal spending but helps a public goal.
- **Strategy**: the player judges when to prioritize private gain versus shared improvement.
- **Systems**: the bridge changes routes, access, work patterns, and future opportunities for many actors.
- **Legacy**: civic investment shapes the kind of town that exists later.

## NPC Trust / Requests Example
- **Wonder**: villagers notice what the player did.
- **Planning**: fulfilling or delaying requests changes later interactions.
- **Strategy**: advice, timing, and trustworthiness must be weighed against each other.
- **Systems**: requests, trade, rumors, and reputation shifts influence decisions under uncertainty.
- **Legacy**: long-term patterns of reliability or selfishness change what social life becomes.
