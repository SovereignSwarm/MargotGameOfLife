# NPC Constitution

## Purpose
NPCs exist to make the village socially legible. They give the player requests, reactions, pressure, reflection, and partial perspective.

## What NPCs Are For
- social texture in daily life
- requests and commissions tied to real needs
- feedback about what changed
- partial perspective rather than perfect truth
- conflicting advice that creates judgment
- memory and consequence over time

## Requests and Commissions
NPCs should connect local needs to work. A baker request, builder request, or warning from a neighbor should point the player toward meaningful decisions, not arbitrary task lists.

## Feedback and Reflection
NPCs should help the player notice:
- what was gained
- what was sacrificed
- what improved
- what was neglected
- what kind of person or household the player is becoming

They should reflect the world back, not summarize the theme at length.

## Partial Perspective and Conflicting Advice
NPCs should not agree all the time and should not know everything. Different villagers should expose trade-offs from their own position, role, and incentives.

## Memory and Consequence
NPC memory should stay simple but meaningful. It should help track patterns such as reliability, selfishness, generosity, delay, and follow-through. Memory exists to support consequence, not to simulate human consciousness.

## What NPCs Are Not For
- exposition dumping
- replacing player judgment
- constant praise
- abstract tutorial narration
- high-complexity AI gimmicks
- becoming the main spectacle instead of the village itself

## Anti-Patterns
- dialogue that explains what the player can already see
- perfect advisors that remove uncertainty
- NPC systems that demand far more complexity than the rest of the game
- turning villagers into content vending machines
