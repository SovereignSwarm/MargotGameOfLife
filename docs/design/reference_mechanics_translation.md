# Reference Mechanics Translation

## Purpose
This document translates strong external design patterns into MargotGameOfLife's own canon, ontology, roadmap, and production language so the team can borrow useful structure without importing whole feature bundles, widening Milestone 1, or drifting away from the game's actual identity.

## Core Rule
Outside references must be translated into Margot's own canon, ontology, roadmap, and current production stage rather than copied directly. Borrow the pattern, then restate it in Margot terms before it is allowed to shape design.

## How To Use This Document
- Use this during future design work, milestone planning, and expansion discussions as a filter, not as a wishlist.
- When a reference idea comes up, map it to Margot's permanent verbs, loop families, ontology categories, scope layers, comprehension tiers, and phase fit before accepting it.
- If the translated form does not produce visible consequence, child readability, additive growth, or private co-op clarity, defer or reject it.
- Treat `Steal Now` as current design guidance, `Steal Later` as phase-bound guidance, and `Do Not Steal` as anti-drift protection.

## Reference Set
- **Minecraft Education**: matters for readable collaboration, host-safe world framing, and clear build-to-learn affordances.
- **Dragon Quest Builders 2**: matters for staged restoration, place-based projects, and visible world improvement through building.
- **Stardew Valley**: matters for a legible gather-make-sell-save rhythm and compact specialist-driven daily life.
- **Animal Crossing**: matters for social texture, place attachment, gentle legibility, and strong host-world framing.
- **My Time at Sandrock**: matters for commission structure, workshop identity, and restoration that changes shared life.
- **Dinkum**: matters for lightweight co-op presence, town-shaping readability, and practical settlement routines without MMO scale.

## Translation By Game Aspect

### Core Loop
- **Best source influences**: Stardew Valley, My Time at Sandrock, Dragon Quest Builders 2.
- **What Margot should steal**: a highly readable gather -> make -> trade -> improve rhythm where work clearly turns into better future options.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: keep the Production Loop, Ownership Loop, and Civic Loop tightly chained so `Gather`, `Make`, `Trade`, `Save`, `Own`, and `Contribute` read as one meaning sequence rather than separate minigames. Early rewards should stay tangible: goods, coins, productive assets, and visible public works.
- **What to avoid copying**: stamina-tax treadmills, profession sprawl, side-loop clutter, and any progression frame where maximizing money becomes the emotional end state.

### World Structure
- **Best source influences**: Dragon Quest Builders 2, Animal Crossing, Dinkum.
- **What Margot should steal**: revisitable places that visibly change, feel locally distinct, and remain meaningful after improvement.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: build future districts as combinations of `Place`, `Project Site`, `Civic Structure`, `Access Structure`, specialist routine, and old work or restorable trace. Expansion should add new local expressions of the same world rather than replace Orchard and Bridge.
- **What to avoid copying**: total-edit sandbox assumptions, town-score abstractions, and flattening every place into a player-owned layout canvas.

### Co-op
- **Best source influences**: Minecraft Education, Dragon Quest Builders 2, Dinkum.
- **What Margot should steal**: visible collaboration in one shared world where players can understand what is shared, what is personal, and what they are working toward together.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: future co-op design should always name the active scope layer: `Personal`, `Shared Household`, or `Town / Civic`. Milestone 1 stays personal plus civic only; Milestone 2 is where the first real household trade-off should arrive. Co-op should read as shared planning and shared consequence, not just synchronized presence.
- **What to avoid copying**: public-server thinking, shared-everything blur, drop-in chaos as default, and the false completion state of "two solo players nearby."

### NPCs and Social Texture
- **Best source influences**: Stardew Valley, My Time at Sandrock, Animal Crossing.
- **What Margot should steal**: specialists with recognizable needs, concise reactions, and everyday texture that makes the village feel socially legible.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: NPCs should mainly act through `Commission`, `Feedback`, partial perspective, and simple memory of follow-through. Their job is to reflect requests, trade-offs, gain, neglect, and visible consequence back to the player.
- **What to avoid copying**: affection grinding, filler chatter, exposition-first dialogue, and character systems that overshadow the village itself.

### Creatures and Wonder
- **Best source influences**: Animal Crossing, Minecraft Education.
- **What Margot should steal**: habitat-tied delight, readable observation, and gentle wonder that makes places feel alive.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: future creatures should be place-bound presences that deepen stewardship, observation, and quiet mystery. They should mostly function as `Observation`, light `Companion`, or place-signaling life rather than as a separate collection ladder.
- **What to avoid copying**: collection meta, combat escalation, rarity-chasing as a primary goal, and roster-completion pressure that pulls focus away from craft, society, and restoration.

### UX and Legibility
- **Best source influences**: Minecraft Education, Animal Crossing, Stardew Valley.
- **What Margot should steal**: obvious affordances, readable stations, simple routes, and progress that can be understood before reading dense UI.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: show clear sources, clear craft sites, clear trade points, clear ownership boundaries, and clear `Project Stage` changes. Wonder-tier readability should come first: a child should be able to point at where value comes from and what changed.
- **What to avoid copying**: menu-first complexity, hidden stats, adult-only optimization surfaces, and progress systems that matter more in UI than in the world.

### Rewards and Motivation
- **Best source influences**: Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, My Time at Sandrock.
- **What Margot should steal**: reward structures based on capability, comfort, beauty, access, routine improvement, and social acknowledgement.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: rewards should cash out as better life: productive assets, easier routes, improved places, stronger routines, and visible appreciation from villagers. This keeps money as fuel and keeps the world response more important than the number increase.
- **What to avoid copying**: streaks, FOMO loops, raw completionism, arbitrary reward fountains, and reward design that makes accumulation feel more meaningful than what accumulation enables.

### Progression and Depth Reveal
- **Best source influences**: Stardew Valley, Dragon Quest Builders 2, My Time at Sandrock.
- **What Margot should steal**: layered reveal where the same verbs stay readable early and deepen over time.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: progression should move through Margot's comprehension tiers: `Wonder`, `Planning`, `Strategy`, `Systems`, `Legacy`. District growth should add sideways depth, new place meaning, and richer judgment without making earlier content spiritually obsolete.
- **What to avoid copying**: hard tech-tree mentality, tutorial-content obsolescence, replacement-tier progression, and complexity that only makes sense after the basics have been mentally discarded.

### Parent / Host Controls
- **Best source influences**: Minecraft Education, Animal Crossing.
- **What Margot should steal**: a safe private-world frame and simple host authority that supports parent-child play without turning the game into admin software.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: future host-control design should favor a small set of host-readable permissions around invites, shared-world boundaries, and use of shared structures. These controls should preserve emotional safety and clarity for private co-op, not simulate public moderation stacks.
- **What to avoid copying**: admin-heavy moderation thinking, punitive child-policing systems, and permission grids so complex they become the real game.

### Restoration, Projects, and Public Goods
- **Best source influences**: Dragon Quest Builders 2, My Time at Sandrock, Animal Crossing.
- **What Margot should steal**: staged visible restoration and durable before-and-after world change that makes contribution feel concrete.
- **How it should concretely translate in Margot**: future public-good design should default to `Project Site -> Project Stage -> Civic Asset / Access Structure`. Contributions should change access, workflow, beauty, or opportunity in a revisitable place. This is the clearest form of `Visible Consequence`.
- **What to avoid copying**: donation voids, cosmetic-only restoration, invisible civic scoring, and public-good systems that cannot be pointed to in the world.

## Steal Now / Steal Later / Do Not Steal

### Steal Now
- Visible project staging as the baseline test for any civic feature.
- Place-first consequence as the default rule for future proposals.
- Concise NPC reflection instead of chatter-heavy social content.
- Child-readable affordance discipline around sources, stations, routes, and outcomes.
- Explicit personal, household, and civic scope framing during design review, even when household play is deferred.

### Steal Later
- Shared household holdings and one real household trade-off.
- District-specialist commission chains that deepen local identity.
- Creature and place attachment layers that reward observation and care.
- Stronger host controls for private co-op boundaries and comfort.
- Bounded information, record, rumor, and trust patterns for later judgment loops.

### Do Not Steal
- Relationship grind loops as a primary motivation structure.
- Collection or completion as the main reason to play.
- Public-online assumptions, stranger-social systems, or MMO framing.
- Opaque economy systems that hide where value comes from or what money changes.
- Feature-bloat settlement sandboxes that dilute the permanent verbs and loop families.

## Fit By Production Phase
- **Foundation Lock / Milestone 0**: use this document as translation vocabulary and anti-drift filtering only. It should help reject imitation and keep future ideas in Margot language.
- **Milestone 1**: only readability steals belong here now: obvious sources and stations, visible project stages, simple reflection, and place-based consequence. No household layer, district expansion, creature system growth, deeper commission webs, or host-control expansion.
- **Milestone 2**: apply shared-life steals here: shared holdings, one real household trade-off, clearer household governance, and co-op meaning beyond shared map presence.
- **Milestone 3**: apply additive-expansion steals here: district identity, specialist content chains, stronger place attachment, restoration arcs, and light creature-place layering that still protects Orchard and Bridge.
- **Milestone 4+**: apply judgment and durable-world steals here: partial information, conflicting advice, records, trust shifts, stronger host-control guidance, and other systems that depend on a more mature readable base.

## Candidate Reusable Patterns
- **Commission pattern**: A villager need becomes a `Commission` that creates a production, timing, or delivery choice and then produces visible follow-up in trade, feedback, or access. This keeps requests relational and systemic instead of turning them into abstract quest logs.
- **Project / restoration pattern**: Shared contribution advances a `Project Site` through visible `Project Stage` changes until it becomes a durable `Civic Asset` or `Access Structure`. This is the cleanest way to keep public goods child-legible and revisitably real.
- **Companion / creature pattern**: A place-bound living presence rewards observation, timing, and care without becoming a separate mastery ladder. The creature exists to deepen wonder, place meaning, and stewardship, not to replace the village loop.
- **Reflection / feedback pattern**: An NPC, place, or visible outcome briefly reflects what was gained, sacrificed, improved, or neglected after a meaningful action. This keeps social and systemic feedback compact, legible, and tied to consequence.
- **Host-control pattern**: A small set of private-world host settings protects safety, clarity, and cooperation without importing public-server administration logic. It should support shared life, not overshadow it.
- **Place-attachment pattern**: Repeated return makes a place more useful, beautiful, familiar, and socially meaningful over time. Attachment should come from care, use, and visible change, not from checklist completion alone.

## Anti-Drift Warnings
- Do not turn reference games into feature checklists. Translate the underlying pattern or reject it.
- Do not smuggle Milestone 2 household meaning or Milestone 3 district growth into Milestone 1 because a reference makes it feel standard.
- Do not import new ontology classes when existing categories such as `Commission`, `Contribution`, `Project`, `Civic Asset`, `Observation`, or `Reputation Shift` already fit.
- Do not let NPC charm, creature charm, or settlement customization outrun visible value creation, ownership, and civic consequence.
- Do not mistake co-op for networked sandbox freedom. Margot is private parent-child co-op, not a public social platform.
- Do not let rewards collapse into money, hearts, rarity, or completion percentages when the game's meaning is better life, better place, and better judgment.
- Do not let later expansion make Orchard and Bridge feel like fake tutorial debris.
- Do not use outside inspiration as cover for lore bloat, system bloat, or UI-heavy abstraction.

## Recommendation
Use this translation as a standing gate for future design proposals. Any proposal that borrows from an outside reference should name the source pattern, state the Margot translation in canon and ontology terms, identify its production-phase fit, and state what is being deliberately left out.

## Final Rule
Translate the pattern, not the game.
